
ECHO‑13: Abstract

ECHO‑13 is a deterministic symbolic language for modeling continuous unfolding through entities, traces, relational dynamics, braid geometry, and non‑destructive Soft Overlap Compression (SOR). Unlike traditional computational models that overwrite state, ECHO‑13 preserves identity and history across time, allowing structure to accumulate rather than reset.

The system defines computation as an evolving geometric and relational process. Entities move through space with tempo, density, and phase; traces record their full motion history; relational distance governs synchrony and pair formation; braids emerge from intertwined traces; and environmental fields respond to motion and crossings. SOR increases density through overlap without destroying prior information, enabling non‑destructive memory growth.

This framework supports research in symbolic computation, emergent behavior, artificial life, and continuous dynamical systems. The reference implementation included in this repository provides a minimal, dependency‑free engine suitable for experimentation, visualization, and theoretical analysis.
